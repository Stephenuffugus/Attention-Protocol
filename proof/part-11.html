21 CFR Part 11 — How SWS Satisfies the Regulation

Clause-by-clause mapping of FDA's Electronic Records and Electronic Signatures regulation to the technical controls in a SWS attention receipt. For pharma compliance, medical education, and GxP training records.

Summary in one sentence: SWS provides the technical controls that 21 CFR Part 11 requires — tamper-evident records, secure audit trails, cryptographic signatures, and device checks. Your organization provides the procedural controls (SOPs, access policies, training). Together they satisfy Part 11 for the "did the trainee actually do the training" class of electronic records.
Choose your timestamp authority. SWS supports both anchoring options, selectable per-receipt:
  • RFC 3161 TSA (default for pharma pilots) — DigiCert, Sectigo, GlobalSign, Entrust, or any CAB-Forum-accredited TSA. Same standard your security team already uses for Authenticode and PAdES-LTV.
  • OpenTimestamps / Bitcoin — decentralized blockchain anchoring. Stronger cryptographic properties but requires your compliance policy to accept blockchain-based timestamps.
  • Both, on the same receipt — belt-and-suspenders for maximum regulatory defensibility. Different failure modes protect each other.
Why this matters now: The FDA issued 327 Part 11-related warning letters in the second half of 2025 alone — a 73% year-over-year increase. Data integrity and audit-trail deficiencies are the most frequently cited findings. "Click-through completion" in pharma training records is increasingly unacceptable under Part 11 inspection.

Subpart B §11.10 — Controls for Closed Systems

ClauseRequirementSWS Control
§11.10(a) Validation of systems to ensure accuracy, reliability, and consistent intended performance. 778 unit + integration tests, 100% pass (2026-04-24); reproducible builds; published git history in public GitHub repo. MIXED
§11.10(b) Ability to generate accurate and complete copies of records in both human-readable and electronic form. Each receipt is emitted as a compact JWT (machine-readable) and a JSON-LD Verifiable Credential (human-readable, W3C-standardized). TECH
§11.10(c) Protection of records to enable accurate and ready retrieval throughout the records retention period. SHA-256 integrity hash over every record. Ed25519 signature binds the record to the issuer. OpenTimestamps anchor proves existence at a specific Bitcoin block. TECH
§11.10(d) Limiting system access to authorized individuals. Firestore auth rules gate receipt retrieval to the subject's own vault; customer-side access-control lists are customer responsibility. MIXED
§11.10(e) Secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails to independently record operator entries and actions. Every behavioral event produces a timestamped entry in the receipt's signal log. Tampering breaks the integrity hash. Two independent timestamp-authority options satisfy "computer-generated" and "independent": RFC 3161 TSA (DigiCert / Sectigo / FreeTSA) or OpenTimestamps Bitcoin anchor — or both simultaneously. TECH
§11.10(f) Operational system checks to enforce permitted sequencing of steps and events. Session phases (read → decide → respond → type → complete) are enforced sequentially by the SDK; the integrity hash covers phase ordering so tampering is detectable. TECH
§11.10(g) Authority checks to ensure only authorized individuals can use the system. Consent attestation block records the user's explicit authorization with timestamp + version. Integrated with customer identity provider for tying a DID to an authenticated learner. MIXED
§11.10(h) Device checks to determine validity of data input. Environmental gate (BotD v2) verifies the browser is a real user agent, not a headless automation tool or emulator. Caught 3/3 Puppeteer profiles in our harness. TECH
§11.10(i) Persons developing/maintaining/using the system have the education, training, and experience. Inventor is patent-filed (USPTO 2026-03-17, SWS-PROV-001). Customer training program is customer's responsibility. PROC
§11.10(j) Written policies holding individuals accountable for actions under their electronic signatures. Customer-side SOP; SWS provides the signature mechanism and verdict record but does not author your compliance policy. PROC
§11.10(k) Appropriate controls over systems documentation. SDK source is versioned (public GitHub); receipt schema is documented in-repo; every deployed artifact has a version-stamped URL. TECH

Subpart B §11.30 — Controls for Open Systems

§11.30 Additional measures (encryption, digital signatures) when records travel over open systems to ensure authenticity, integrity, and confidentiality. Ed25519 digital signatures (RFC 8032) over every receipt. Public key published at /.well-known/attention-pubkey.json. Anyone can verify offline with only the public key. TECH

Subpart C §11.50 — Signature Manifestations

§11.50(a) Printed name of the signer. Receipt carries issuer.name = "SWS Strategic Media LLC" and pseudonymous subject DID. Full learner name (if privacy policy permits) resolvable via customer identity provider. TECH
§11.50(b) Date and time of signature. ISO 8601 timestamp in the JWT iat claim + the receipt generated_at. OpenTimestamps Bitcoin anchor provides independent timestamp proof that survives signer compromise. TECH
§11.50(c) The meaning (review, approval, responsibility, authorship) associated with the signature. Credential type AttentionVerificationCredential + explicit verdict field (e.g. verified_human_active_engagement) + human-readable note on every attestation layer. TECH

Subpart C §11.70 — Signature/Record Linking

§11.70 Electronic signatures shall be linked to their respective electronic records to ensure the signatures cannot be excised, copied, or transferred to falsify another record. The Ed25519 signature is computed over the entire JWT payload (which contains the full receipt). Copying the signature to any other payload produces a mismatched hash and fails verification immediately. TECH

Subpart C §11.100 — General Requirements for Electronic Signatures

§11.100(a) Electronic signatures shall be unique to one individual and shall not be reused by, or reassigned to, anyone else. Each learner's subject DID (did:sws:user:<hash>) is deterministically derived and cryptographically unique. Issuer key rotation is tracked via kid in the JWKS. TECH
§11.100(b) Before using e-signatures, the organization shall verify the identity of the individual. Customer identity provider (e.g. SSO / your LMS authentication) binds the authenticated learner to the DID. SWS does not perform identity proofing itself. PROC

Subpart C §11.200 — Electronic Signature Components and Controls

§11.200(a) Non-biometric signatures require at least two distinct identification components (e.g. ID + password). Customer identity provider handles authentication (1st factor) + SWS captures behavioral biometrics during the session (2nd factor, in the §11.200(b) sense). MIXED
§11.200(b) Biometric electronic signatures shall be designed to ensure they cannot be used by anyone other than their genuine owners. 23-signal behavioral composite (Fitts' Law, Hick's Law, prewhitened DFA-1 1/f scaling per Torre-Delignières 2008, two-thirds power law, reading-speed coherence, keystroke digraph timing, submovement count per Crossman-Goodeve 1983 / Meyer 1988, microsaccades per Engbert-Kliegl 2003, 1/f cross-channel coherence per Sklar-Nandakumar formal grounding, etc.) is a behavioral biometric. Composition Integrity (paste-burst + LLM-cheating detector) further detects LLM-assisted submissions. Research basis: arXiv 2511.12468 (97–99% F1 on LLM-assisted cheating). TECH

Subpart C §11.300 — Controls for Identification Codes and Passwords

§11.300 Uniqueness of ID/password combinations, periodic revision, loss management, transaction safeguards, device testing. Password lifecycle is the customer identity provider's responsibility. SWS contributes tamper-evident session records that a customer audit trail can reference. PROC

What This Does NOT Cover

21 CFR Part 11 is a joint tech + process regulation. SWS delivers the technical controls; your organization's written procedures deliver the rest. Specifically outside SWS scope: identity proofing (§11.100(b)), accountability policies (§11.10(j)), periodic password revision (§11.300), internal training records for your QA staff (§11.10(i)), and the system validation master plan (§11.10(a) — we provide supporting evidence; you own the plan). A full Part 11 implementation typically requires an IQ/OQ/PQ validation package referencing the SWS technical controls above.

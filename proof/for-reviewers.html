If you've been asked to review this work, this page is the entry point.

SWS Attention Protocol is a cryptographic receipt of human attention — a SHA-256-anchored, content-bound, tamper-evident record of what happened in a session. Not a confidence score to trust. An audit artifact a regulator, auditor, or court can verify offline using only a public key and standard crypto primitives.

It's not a CAPTCHA, not a classifier. The receipt-as-product framing is the whole point: behavioral classifiers fail against sophisticated bots — receipts don't. Even when the bot detector misses, the receipt is still content-bound, tamper-tested at 100/100, and audit-grade.

  1. Is the receipt-as-product framing actually distinct from "another bot detector"?   2. Is the conformal Bayesian P(human) honestly disclosed? The calibration set is N=5 humans + N=28 bots (v2 as of 2026-04-27; v1 was N=5+10) — still small on the human side. The methodology doc walks through what would and wouldn't survive a stats-class reviewer (SD-floor 0.05 rationale, Gaussian-likelihood-ratio choice, bootstrap CI limitations). Tell me what I missed.
  3. Is the structural single-signal motor limit framed correctly? I claim Bezier mouse trajectories with random control points + 60Hz sampling + ~3px Gaussian jitter produce statistics inside the human-typical band on every individual motor signal we measure (Two-Thirds Power Law β, Cursor Jerk CV, Curvature Index, velocity bell-shape ratio, microsaccade frequency). I claim per-signal threshold tightening cannot fix this and the layered stack is the answer. Push back on either claim.

What's already disclosed (so you can skip checking these)

Limits and weaknesses I've already surfaced in the docs

  • Behavioral-only gap to DMTG-class adversary is 0.131 (engaged human 0.658 vs DMTG 0.55), narrower than the published "0.273 against standard four-bot harness" figure. The 0.65 CME pass threshold catches both with little behavioral-only margin. Disclosed in §0 and §2.5 of the deep dive.
  • Per-signal motor threshold tightening has been tried and reverted twice — Two-Thirds Power Law SD-band tightening and Cursor Jerk Gaussian-decay scoring both made the bot more credible, not less. Documented in §2 of the deep dive and the structural-limit section.
  • Two of the 23 signals carry weight 0 (`oneOverFCoherence`, `microsaccades`) — diagnostic-only because their false-positive rates against curve-aware adversaries haven't earned them weight yet. The receipt surfaces all 23 score values plus a `signalActive` map. Disclosed in §2.
  • Stealth puppeteer on real-hardware can still slip past the 0.50 env-gate threshold at suspicion 0.30 (chrome.runtime missing 0.10 + AudioContext audioWorklet missing 0.20). The audio probe is a real new defensive layer (validated 2026-04-26 evening — naive Puppeteer hits 0.75, stealth Puppeteer hits 0.30) but on real hardware it does not single-handedly catch the most-sophisticated adversary. The defense-in-depth story is honest about this.
  • Honeypot canary detection rate is unbenchmarked. The architectural construction (CSS-hidden + zero-width-chars + HTML-comment redundancy) is sound but we have not run a published-dataset benchmark. Next-quarter work. Disclosed in §4.
  • Calibration human side is small (N=5). Bot side expanded to N=28 in v2 (2026-04-27) with 14 dmtg-bot + 4 stealth-bot real captured runs; human-side expansion waits on a Firestore export of recent legitimate sessions. SD floor 0.05 prevents over-confident classification at small N; bootstrap CI surfaces sample-size uncertainty. The methodology doc walks through this in detail.
  • Bot population is ours. A real-world adversary not in our calibration set might score elsewhere. The receipt's `p_human` reflects the calibration we have, not an absolute truth claim.
  • Patent is a provisional filed 2026-03-17, must convert to utility by 2026-09-17 with $3-5K conversion cost. 247 claims drafted but not yet defended in prosecution.

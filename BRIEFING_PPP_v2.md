## 5. The market in 90 seconds

CME = Continuing Medical Education. Physicians need ~50 credits/year for license renewal. Most credits are earned via **IME** (Independent Medical Education) — pharma-funded accredited educational activities. US IME spend: roughly **$700M–$1B/year**, concentrated in 5–15 large MECs (Medical Education Companies) plus dozens of small specialty MECs like PPP.

```
ACCME (the accreditor)
  ↓ accredits
MECs like PPP, Medscape, DKBmed, Prova
  ↓ propose to
Pharma grantors (Pfizer, Merck, AbbVie, Otsuka, Lundbeck, etc.)
  ↓ fund educational activities for
Physicians (need credits for license)
```

### What's broken

Completion = "watched a video, clicked through, got credit." Click-through fraud has always existed. Now AI-assisted completion (ChatGPT answering post-tests) is the new exposure. Grantors are responding by scoring "measurement of learner progression" harder. The MEC that can write *"per-activity attention-integrity receipt, independently verifiable by your auditor"* on its proposal beats the MEC that writes *"post-test delta and NPS."*

### Why nobody owns this lane

- **BioCatch** — behavioral fraud detection (banks). Doesn't ship a signed audit artifact, just a score.
- **Roundtable (YC W26)** — proof-of-humanness scoring API. Same lane, same gap (no signed receipt).
- **Medscape, Pfizer's internal teams** — no one is producing cryptographic, offline-verifiable completion receipts for CME.

You're **not selling against an existing solution** — you're selling into an unowned compliance gap that grantor pressure is opening up right now.

## 9. The 10 discovery questions (in order)

Cheap context first, expensive context last. Listen more than you talk.

1. **"Walk me through how you currently fill the 'measurement of learner progression' section in an Otsuka or AbbVie psychiatry IME proposal — what does that look like today?"** — opening question; tells you exactly how their proposal-writing works and what they currently cite.
2. **"What's changed in grantor expectations on that section in the last 12–18 months?"** — confirms or refutes the Pfizer-2025 / ACCME-Dec-2025 narrative. If "nothing has changed," your wedge is wrong for them; keep going.
3. **"Last losing proposal — what did the 'measurement' section look like, and what would the winning version have needed?"** — most valuable answer in the call. Quote in their own words; this becomes your pitch for the next 5 calls.
4. **"How is your team currently thinking about AI-assisted post-test completion — physicians using ChatGPT to answer questions?"** — surfaces the AI-assist fear. "We're not worried" = lying or unusually relaxed; "this is the conversation we're having" = bingo.
5. **"When ACCME comes for the next audit, what evidence do you produce that the credits you awarded were earned by real engagement and not click-through?"** — forces them to articulate their current evidence stack. Honest answer is "that's procedural, not cryptographic" — your opening.
6. **"In a typical accredited activity, what % of completers are flagged as suspicious by your current QA process — and what happens to them?"** — gets you their false-positive tolerance. Your answer to "what's your FPR" depends on theirs.
7. **"Have you ever evaluated BioCatch, Roundtable, or any 'human verification' vendor? What killed it or kept it alive?"** — names the competitive landscape; their answer tells you what objections to expect.
8. **"Putting aside vendor and integration cost — if I told you tomorrow that every accredited activity emitted a signed cryptographic receipt your auditor could verify offline against our published key, what's the first thing your compliance officer would ask?"** — hypothetical lets you surface objections without committing. Their first follow-up = your hardest objection to prepare for.
9. **"Where does this conversation go inside PPP if it goes anywhere — who else is in the room before a measurement pilot gets approved?"** — maps the buying committee. Solo Director-discretion = fast path. CEO-required = 6-month path.
10. **"What would you need to see in the first two weeks of a pilot to know it was worth the next two?"** — conversion question. If they have an answer, you're talking to a real buyer. Pause + "good question, let me think" = they don't yet, also useful.

# TEAM 2: MARKET RESEARCH & SURVEY PLATFORMS

## THE OPPORTUNITY

The market research industry has an $81 billion annual revenue stream built on a foundation that everyone in the industry knows is cracked. Research Defender, one of the leading fraud prevention tools, reports that standard survey sources have a 33% fraud rate and even top-tier sources run at 26%. Worse, 84% of that fraud looks "too good to clean" — meaning it passes traditional quality checks. The industry's own trade associations have called data quality an "existential issue."

Current solutions (Research Defender, Imperium, Verisoul, PureSpectrum) focus on catching bad actors through device fingerprinting, IP analysis, and behavioral pattern matching at the respondent level. They answer the question: "Is this a real person?"

The SWS protocol answers a fundamentally better question: "Did this real person actually pay attention to the survey?" A respondent can be a verified real human AND still speed-click through a survey in 90 seconds. Current tools miss that entirely. We catch it.

## WHAT WE SELL THEM

A JavaScript tag they drop on their survey platform. For every survey response, they get an Attention Quality Score (0-100) based on:

- How long the respondent spent on each question (duration_ms)
- How many interactions they made per question (interaction_count)
- Whether their timing patterns match genuine cognitive processing (timing entropy, Hick's Law compliance)
- Whether their scrolling behavior shows actual reading (saccade-fixation analysis)
- Whether their response timing scales with question complexity (cognitive micro-pauses)

The output is a per-response quality score that flags: "This response came from a verified human who spent 8 minutes reading questions carefully with a Focus Score of 74" vs. "This response came from a human who speed-clicked through in 45 seconds with a Focus Score of 12."

# TEAM 1: INSURANCE & NURSING HOMES

## THE OPPORTUNITY

### Insurance
Insurance companies offer premium discounts to policyholders who complete safety training — defensive driving courses, workplace safety modules, health and wellness programs. The problem: $2-4 billion per year in fraudulent training claims. Policyholders click "complete" without engaging. The insurance company has no way to verify genuine engagement.

Current "proof" of completion: a timestamp showing the module was opened and a checkbox showing it was marked complete. That proves nothing about engagement.

### Nursing Homes
There are approximately 15,000 nursing homes in the US serving 1.3 million residents. Federal regulations (CMS, OSHA, state health departments) require ongoing staff training in infection control, fall prevention, abuse prevention, medication administration, and HIPAA compliance. Nursing homes face fines of $10,000-$100,000+ per violation when training is found to be inadequate during state surveys.

Current training compliance: Learning management systems (Relias, HealthStream, CareAcademy) track login times and quiz scores. Staff click through videos at 4x speed and pass simple quizzes using common sense. The training was "completed" but no learning occurred.

This is personal for you, Stephen. You've spent time in nursing homes. You've seen what happens when staff aren't properly trained. Your protocol can prove who actually paid attention to fall prevention training and who clicked through. That's not just a business opportunity — that's protecting vulnerable people.

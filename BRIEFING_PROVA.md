# Discovery Call Briefing: Prova Education
**Practice round 3. Phone-readable. Read 5 min before the call.**

---

## Who they are (30 seconds)

- Mid-size hematology-oncology MEC
- Right-sized: 60-day pilot is Director-discretionary
- Independent (not PE-rolled)
- Last practice round before strategic accounts

## Who you're targeting

**Director of Outcomes** or **Director of Educational Strategy** (verify on LinkedIn before sending).

Mid-size org → Director-level is the right altitude. You don't need the President here. You do need the person who actually writes the "measurement" section of the IME proposal.

## Why Prova third (the practice round 3 logic)

PPP teaches the open. CEC teaches the cardiology pivot. Prova is the dress rehearsal for hem-onc, which is the highest-grant-dollar specialty. By Prova you've sharpened twice; you walk in with a much better answer to "what's your false-positive rate?" than you had on Tuesday.

## The hook (memorize this sentence)

> *"You're competing against MECs 5x your headcount for the same hem-onc Pfizer pool. The rubric is no longer 'we measure outcomes,' every MEC says that. The differentiator is what specifically you measure and how a grantor can verify it."*

That sentence acknowledges Prova's competitive reality (mid-size vs giants like Medscape, DKBmed) and reframes the differentiator from "we measure outcomes" to "we produce verifiable artifacts."

## The first-meeting ask

> *"Show me a redacted recent IME proposal's 'measurement' section so I can see what would actually move the needle."*

This is the boldest first-meeting ask. Almost everyone says no. The 10% who say yes are the 10% you want to pilot with. The 90% who say no still appreciate the concreteness of the question.

If they say no, the fallback ask:

> *"Walk me through what your current 'measurement of learner progression' paragraph looks like at a high level. I won't ask for the redacted doc."*

## The risk to watch for

**"We don't have engineering headcount."** This is the #1 objection from mid-size MECs. They're worried about integration cost.

Pre-empt it:

> *"One script tag. The evidence kit self-qualifies in 10 minutes. Your team writes init/render/decision/completion glue, about 30 lines, plus a template injection. I deliver the SDK, the xAPI adapter, the verifier, the JWKS, and 10 hours synchronous Slack support during the pilot. Production rollout is 2-4 engineer-weeks; the first receipt is 1-3 engineer-days. If you can spare 1 engineer-week of attention from someone, I make sure that week is enough."*

That paragraph is in OUTREACH_PLAY.md section 3 (Integration playbook, Archetype 1). Memorize the rough shape.

## The hardest question they will ask

**Q: "What's your false-positive rate?"**

Honest answer (refined from PPP and CEC):

> *"On the gated composite against 4 standard adversarial bot profiles, every bot caps at 0.30. Engaged humans land 0.55-0.66. Gap 0.24-0.27. Against curve-aware Bezier-class bots the behavioral signal alone narrows to ~0.13. The env-gate and composition layer carry the difference. I haven't run it on a real hem-onc CME population. That's literally the pilot question."*

Bridge phrase:

> *"What FPR would your team treat as the line? What would force you to remediate manually vs. accept?"*

## The hem-onc-specific concern to anticipate

**Pharma grantor sophistication.** Hem-onc is where the grantors (Pfizer, Merck, BMS, AstraZeneca) have the most analytical sophistication. They're going to ask Prova hard questions about evidence quality. Prova knows this.

Frame the receipt as their "level-up" answer:

> *"When a Pfizer hem-onc reviewer asks 'how do you know learners actually engaged with the activity, given that AI-assisted post-tests are now a documented concern,' your current answer is post-test deltas + NPS. Your competitor's answer is the same. The next reviewer cycle, the winning answer is going to be 'here's the cryptographic receipt the learner generated, and here's the link your team can verify offline.' I'm betting the receipt is what flips a tied proposal."*

## Never-say in this call

- "Medscape / DKBmed are way ahead of you" (true comparatively but condescending)
- "AI is the threat" (be specific: AI-assisted post-tests, not "AI")
- "We just need 10 minutes of your engineer's time" (mid-size MECs hear this and roll their eyes)

## Always-say in this call

- "The rubric is harder. Your faculty depth is the same." (validates their reality)
- "I'd want your team telling me which of the flagged sessions are real misses." (offers them the validator role, not the customer role)
- "What's your read on the Pfizer-rubric trend? Am I right about it?" (gets them to teach you, which they appreciate)

## The 60-day pilot ask

Same shape as PPP and CEC, but with hem-onc-specific framing:
- 1 accredited hem-onc activity (their choice)
- ≤5,000 learner-sessions
- 60 days
- Free for them; you co-author a case study they can put on their website AND in their next Pfizer hem-onc proposal
- One script tag in the activity-template HTML

The "case study they can use in their next Pfizer proposal" is the real value-trade. They get rubric ammunition. You get a real-data datapoint.

## After the call

Same post-call discipline. Particularly important after Prova: by now you have THREE call deltas to compare. That's how you sharpen for DKBmed and PeerView, the strategic accounts.

What to look for in the deltas:
- Which language about cryptographic verification landed and which didn't?
- Which specialty-specific framing got nods and which got blank stares?
- Which 60-day pilot framing got "yes maybe" and which got "we'd need procurement to look at it first"?

If procurement comes up at Prova, that's a flag for DKBmed (similar size). If procurement does NOT come up at Prova, that's a green light for Director-direct outreach to DKBmed.

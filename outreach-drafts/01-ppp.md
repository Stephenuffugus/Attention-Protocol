# Outreach Draft #1 — Physicians Postgraduate Press (PPP)

**Status:** ready to personalize and send. Practice round #1 per `OUTREACH_PLAY.md`.
**Position:** psychiatry-focused MEC + journal publisher (J Clinical Psychiatry).
**Why first:** smallest team, lighter vendor review, lowest cost if the open lands wrong. Specialty-relevance angle is real (psychiatry is the only specialty where engagement integrity is clinically meaningful, not just compliance).
**Risk to dodge:** sounding like behavioral surveillance to a privacy-skeptical psych audience. Lead with no-PII / no-content / no-URLs in sentence one of the body.

---

## Step 1 — Find the right human (LinkedIn, ~10 minutes)

1. Open LinkedIn, search: `Physicians Postgraduate Press`
2. Click the company page → "People" tab
3. Filter by title — look for, in priority order:
   - **Director of Accreditation**
   - **Director of CME** / Director of Continuing Medical Education
   - **Director of Outcomes** / Director of CME Outcomes
   - **VP Education** / VP CME
   - If none of the above: **Managing Editor** of the J Clinical Psychiatry CME unit (often holds the accreditation function in smaller publisher-MECs)
4. Pick the person whose profile shows: tenure ≥ 2 years at PPP, posts/articles indicating active engagement (not a placeholder profile), and a public-facing email pattern (often `firstinitiallastname@ppponline.com` or similar — verify the domain by visiting their company site).
5. **If LinkedIn shows no clear match,** look up J Clinical Psychiatry's masthead page on the journal website — accreditation contacts are often listed there.
6. Note the person's name, title, and (if visible) two specific recent things they posted/published — you'll use one of them as the personalization hook in line 2.

**Do NOT send if you cannot verify the person actually holds the role.** Cold-emailing the wrong human burns the account.

---

## Step 2 — Pick a subject line

Three options, ranked by my best guess at reply rate:

1. **"Customer development — psychiatry-CME measurement question"** ← recommended
2. "Solo founder, patent filed — 20 minutes on psych-CME engagement evidence?"
3. "Learning conversation: what grantors are pushing on in psych IME"

The first is honest about the frame ("customer development"), specific to their world ("psychiatry-CME"), and avoids selling-tells like "quick chat?" or "exploring partnership." The second is more attention-grabbing but flirts with sounding pitchy. The third is the safest.

**Why every line is in there:**

## Step 5 — When to send

- **Day:** Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday. Avoid Monday (inbox overload) and Friday (weekend wipe).
- **Time:** 8:00–10:00 AM their timezone. PPP is Memphis-based (Central) → send 9:00–11:00 AM ET works.

## Step 6 — Reply decision tree

### A. No reply within 7 days
**Send a one-line bump:**

> [First name] — light bump in case the first one buried. No worries either way.
>
> — Stephen

If still no reply after the bump (~14 days from initial), **drop them. Move on.** Persistent re-pinging at this stage is more costly than the missed account.

### B. Reply: "Send me materials first"
This is a soft yes. They want to self-qualify before spending 20 minutes. Reply same-day with:
- Architecture 1-pager (Task #35, write this WEEK)
- Privacy DPIA + sub-processor list (Task #37)
- 21 CFR Part 11 clause-by-clause matrix (already exists at `proof/part-11.html`)
- Live demo URL + public verifier URL
- Receipt JWT sample paste-block

Keep the ask warm: *"Happy to walk through any of these. The 20-minute ask still stands once you've had a look."*

### C. Reply: "Let's talk"
This is the win. Reply within 4 hours.

1. Propose 3 specific 30-min slots (give them choice of times — never "let me know what works").
2. Once booked, pre-call ritual:
   - Re-read `OUTREACH_PLAY.md §5` (the 12 questions) — drill them aloud.
   - Re-read `OUTREACH_PLAY.md §6` (mock transcript) — note where Marcus surprised "Stephen" (the AI-assisted-completion line). Be ready for a similar surprise.
   - Have demo open in one tab, verify.html in another, the PPP company page in a third.
   - **Do not have a slide deck.** This is a learning conversation; bring questions, not assertions.
3. Day-of:
   - Hit the **anti-pitch opening** (`OUTREACH_PLAY.md §4 (c)`).
   - Listen more than you talk in minutes 1–10.
   - When they ask one of the 12 questions, give the *honest* answer — never the wrong one. Bridge back to learning.
   - Close with: *"What would you need to see in the first two weeks of a pilot to know it was worth the next two?"* — that's the question that converts a learning conversation into a forward step.
4. Within 30 minutes of hangup, run the post-call discipline (`OUTREACH_PLAY.md §8`).

### D. Reply: "Not interested" / "Wrong person"
Fine. Reply: *"Appreciated. If you can point me at who DOES own that question at PPP, I'd be grateful."* That's worth doing — they often forward you internally.

### E. Reply: a question or pushback
**Don't sell. Answer the question.** If they ask "how is this different from BioCatch," use the honest answer from `OUTREACH_PLAY.md §5 Q1`. If they ask "are you SOC 2," use Q4. Treat each reply as the first 90 seconds of the eventual call.

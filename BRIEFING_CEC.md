# Discovery Call Briefing: Creative Educational Concepts (CEC)
**Practice round 2. Phone-readable. Read 5 min before the call.**

---

## Who they are (30 seconds)

- Cardiology-weighted boutique MEC
- Independent (boutique = no PE / no procurement layer / founder owns the call)
- Cardiology IME = the most competitive grant pool in pharma-funded CME
- Sharp "differentiate or commoditize" pain (everyone says they measure outcomes; faculty depth is no longer the wedge)

## Who you're targeting

**President / Founder.** Boutique → no procurement layer → the founder owns the call.

Verify their name on LinkedIn before sending. CEC's site (cecme.com or similar) usually lists the founder on an About page.

## Why CEC second (the practice round 2 logic)

After PPP loosens up the muscle, CEC sharpens it. Cardiology faculty are tech-skeptical. The pitch has to pivot. You are practicing the pivot.

## The hook (memorize this sentence)

> *"Cardiology IME is the most competitive category in pharma-funded CME. CEC wins on faculty depth, but the rubric now rewards measurement rigor your faculty depth doesn't fill in."*

That sentence flatters their existing strength (faculty depth) while naming the new pressure (measurement-rigor rubric). Lands clean to a founder who's been writing IME proposals for years.

## The first-meeting ask

> *"What does your last losing cardiology proposal look like in the 'measurement' section? And what would the winning version have needed?"*

That question gets them venting about a real loss. It also positions you as a learner, not a seller. It almost always produces 3-5 minutes of useful intel.

## The risk to watch for

**Cardiology faculty are tech-skeptical. Don't open with behavioral-science jargon.** No Fitts/Hick/fractal-1/f language in the first 5 minutes.

**Lead with cryptographic integrity.** That word lands cleanly with cardiologists who appreciate concrete artifacts (they sign chart notes, prescriptions, attestations daily). "Cryptographically signed receipt" reads as "auditable artifact." That's the right mental model for cardiology.

## The hardest question they will ask

**Q: "How is this different from BioCatch / Roundtable?"**

Honest answer (compressed for cardiology audience):

> *"BioCatch is the leading behavioral fraud detector. They classify, they output a score. Roundtable is the YC-backed proof-of-humanness lane. We don't compete on classifier accuracy. We ship a signed audit artifact your grantor can verify offline six months later without calling us. Different artifact entirely. The closest analogy in your world is the difference between a lab result and a signed chart note. We're the chart note."*

The chart-note analogy will land in cardiology. Borrow it.

Bridge phrase:

> *"Has your team ever evaluated BioCatch or Roundtable? What killed it or kept it alive?"*

## The hardest specific objection

**Q: "Pharma compliance teams already trust our outcomes data. Why do they need cryptographic verification?"**

Honest answer:

> *"They trusted you in 2019. The 2024-2025 RFP cycle scored measurement evidence explicitly. Pfizer in particular. The trust is still there, but the rubric is harder. The receipt is for the rubric, not the trust. It's a paragraph in your next proposal that no other MEC can write yet."*

## Never-say in this call

- "BioCatch sucks" (they don't, you'd lose credibility)
- Behavioral-science jargon in the first 5 minutes (Fitts, Hick, microsaccades, fractal scaling)
- "We're going to revolutionize CME" (boutique founders hear this constantly and it's a tell)

## Always-say in this call

- "Cryptographically signed receipt" (lands clean in cardiology)
- "Your faculty depth is real. The rubric just changed underneath it." (acknowledges their strength)
- "I'd rather your team validate the claim than take it from me." (offers the pilot framing)

## The 60-day pilot ask

Same as PPP:
- 1 accredited cardiology activity
- ≤5,000 learner-sessions
- 60 days
- Free for them; you co-author the case study
- One script tag in the activity-template HTML
- Their QA team self-verifies every receipt

## After the call

Same post-call discipline as PPP. Follow-up by Friday with whatever they asked for. Compare the deltas from PPP and CEC: which language landed, which didn't. Adjust before Prova.

# Outreach Play — SWS Attention Protocol

**Origin:** 2026-04-27. Four research agents (sales-development analyst, enterprise-software CFO, solutions architect, sales-trainer) ran in parallel against the existing dossier set, primer files, technical-FAQ, and SDK source. This doc is the synthesis. Stephen runs from here.

**Posture:** *learning conversations, not sales calls.* No SOC 2, no MSA, no insurance, no entity formalized today — those are gates to **paid F500 contracts**, not gates to talking. A learning conversation needs an email address. Period.

**One-line frame to repeat to yourself before every call:** *"I built something I think solves a problem this person has. I haven't earned the right to assume that yet. I'm here to learn whether the problem I'm targeting matches their reality."*

### The shaky common assumption across all 5

**Causal attribution from "verified receipt exists" → "P&L line moves."** In 4 of 5 (Medscape, DKBmed, PlatformQ direct, Credly), the value math hinges on win-rate lift — winning incremental grants or recapturing lost deals — and a 60-day pilot cannot prove that. It can prove the receipt verifies, the integration works, and one customer values it. In the 5th (Pfizer), the value is insurance against a tail event that is by definition unobservable on a pilot timeline.

**Be ready to defend with:** (a) named pilot success criteria that are *artifact-quality* metrics, not revenue metrics; (b) the honest "the pilot proves the artifact; the business case is contingent on Year-2 evidence" line — said *before* the buyer says it; (c) the Pfizer case priced as named-module-only so production economics pencil above 3x. The PlatformQ platform-feature lever and the Credly per-credential uplift are the two cleanest ROIs because the value mechanism is testable in real RFPs/deals during the pilot window — those should LEAD the conversation when both sides of the house are open.

## 3. Integration playbook — what testing actually entails for them

### Archetype 1: CME provider running an LMS (Medscape, DKBmed, PlatformQ)
- **Embed:** script tag in the activity-template HTML. SCORM/xAPI-CMI5 packaged content — drop `<script src=".../sdk/attention-protocol.min.js">` into the package's `index.html`. Articulate Rise / Captivate use "trigger JavaScript" hook. Moodle/D2L: ship as LTI 1.3 tool wrapping the activity iframe.
- **End-to-end test:** host `proof/cme-demo.html` behind their tenant DNS → one staff learner runs flow → inspect JWT in console → paste into hosted `verify.html` (7 cards green) → confirm xAPI statement landed in their LRS → run `scripts/verify-receipt.js` against `attention-pubkey.json` for offline path.
- **First receipt:** 1-3 engineer-days. **Production rollout:** 2-4 engineer-weeks.
- **Stephen delivers:** minified SDK, xapi-adapter, demo reference, API spec, verify.html, JWKS URL, 10 hours synchronous Slack support, pilot success criteria paste-block.
- **Buyer writes:** init/render/decision/completion glue (~30 lines), template injection, LRS extension wiring, dashboard query.
- **Pilot ceiling:** 1 activity, ≤5,000 learner-sessions, 60 days.

### Archetype 2: Pharma GxP training (Pfizer, Sanofi, Roche)
- **Embed:** identical to archetype 1. **Procurement is the hard part.** Pre-pilot artifacts demanded: architecture 1-pager (NEEDS WRITING), SOC 2 (DOES NOT EXIST — substitute risk-acceptance memo path), Privacy DPIA (NEEDS WRITING), sub-processor list (NEEDS WRITING), DPA (DOES NOT EXIST).
- **First receipt:** 5-10 engineer-days. **Production rollout:** 3-6 months — SOC 2 / risk-acceptance memo gates expansion, not engineering.

Mitigation: server-side scoring (HARDENING_PLAN T1-1), receipt-bound audience/expiry (T1-2), MSA + insurance (T1-5). Until then, **pilot only on internal-employee training, not customer-facing.**

### Archetype 3: Credentialing platform (Credly, Accredible, BadgeCert)
- **Embed:** they do NOT embed our SDK. Issuer upstream embeds it; Credly *consumes the receipt* as `evidence` field of an OpenBadges 3.0 AchievementCredential. `VC.fromReceipt(receipt)` in `src/sdk/verifiable-credentials.js` produces conformant VC-JWT they ingest unchanged.

- **Hardest objection:** *"What if SWS dies?"* Honest answer: JWKS is a static file Credly can mirror; signatures are mathematical facts; Bitcoin anchors are independent. **Receipts in already-issued badges remain verifiable forever.**

### Archetype 4: Market research panel (Forsta, Cint, Lucid)

- **Pilot ceiling:** 1 study × ≤50,000 completes × 60 days. Above that, signing infra needs paid commitment.
- **Hardest objection:** *"We already use BioCatch / TrustInsights / Imperium."* Those are scoring services. SWS is a **receipt** — a signed artifact the panel hands to the *end-buyer of the data*, not a black-box score the panel consumes.

### Archetype 5: Survey/form platform marketplace (SurveyMonkey, Qualtrics, Typeform)
- **Embed:** marketplace plugin / power-up. **Reality check:** none of these accept plugins from entities without ToS, privacy policy, DPA, often SOC 2. **Marketplace gating is procurement, not engineering.** Defer until ToS/privacy/DPA exist.

## 4. The pitch — three openings

### (a) Concise (60 words)
> "[Name], thanks for the 20 minutes. I'm Stephen Furpahs, solo founder of SWS Attention Protocol — patent-filed March 17. We issue cryptographically-signed receipts that prove a real human paid attention to a screen, verifiable offline by your auditor. I'm not here to sell you anything today. I want to learn whether the problem I built this for matches what's actually keeping you up at night."

**Worst mistake in 90 seconds:** saying "we" when describing the team. They'll ask "how many of you?" within 4 minutes. If "we" lands first and "I'm solo" lands second, you look evasive. **Use "I" from sentence one.**

### (b) Story (120 words)
> "[Name], thanks for the time. I'm Stephen — solo founder, SWS Attention Protocol. Quick scene: a Pfizer IME reviewer is comparing three MEC proposals next quarter. All three say *post-test delta, NPS, 90-day retention.* One of them has an extra paragraph: *'each accredited activity emits an Ed25519-signed receipt, Bitcoin-anchored, independently verifiable against our published JWK.'* That's a sentence the other two literally cannot write. I built a receipt that makes it true — patent filed, code live, public verifier you can paste a JWT into right now. I'm doing customer development this month. I'd rather learn from you whether that paragraph is actually worth anything to a VP Accreditation than guess. Twenty minutes — what's the real picture on your side?"

**Worst mistake:** telling the Pfizer-reviewer story as if it already happened. **Frame it as *"here's the scenario I'm betting on"* — hypothesis, not war story.**

### (c) Anti-pitch (80 words) — RECOMMENDED FOR PRACTICE ROUNDS 1-3
> "[Name], I'll be straight — this is a learning conversation, not a pitch. I built something I think solves a problem CME has, but I haven't earned the right to assume that yet. So I'd love to spend our 20 minutes mostly on your side of the table. What are grantors actually pushing on in IME proposals right now that they weren't pushing on two years ago? I'll answer anything you want about what I built, but I'd rather you teach me first."

**Worst mistake:** asking that question and then *interrupting his answer* to relate it back to your product. **Shut up. Let him finish. Take notes.** The whole point of (c) is that you used it as a Trojan horse if you can't sustain the discipline.

## 5. The 12 questions they will ask

For each: **Honest answer / Wrong answer / Bridge phrase back to learning.**

**Q1. "How is this different from BioCatch / Roundtable?"**
- *Honest:* "BioCatch is best-in-class behavioral fraud detection — a classifier, not a receipt. Roundtable is YC-backed proof-of-human, similar lane. We don't compete on classifier accuracy; we ship a signed, layered receipt your auditor can verify offline six months from now without calling us. Different artifact."
- *Wrong:* "We're more accurate than BioCatch." (You aren't. They have ~2000 features.)
- *Bridge:* "Has your team ever evaluated BioCatch or anything in that lane? What killed it or kept it alive?"

**Q2. "What's your false-positive rate?"**
- *Honest:* "On the gated composite against 4 standard adversarial bot profiles, every bot caps at 0.30; engaged humans land 0.55-0.66 — gap 0.24-0.27. Against curve-aware Bezier-class bots the behavioral signal alone narrows to ~0.13; the env-gate and composition layer carry the difference. I haven't run it on a real CME population — that's the pilot question."
- *Wrong:* "Below 1%." (You don't know.)
- *Bridge:* "What FPR would your team treat as the line — what would force you to remediate manually vs. accept?"

**Q3. "How big is your customer base?"**
- *Honest:* "Zero paying customers. I'm in customer development. The reason I'm calling and not selling is I'd rather have my first three pilots be people who shaped the product than people I oversold."
- *Wrong:* "We're working with several enterprises." (False.)
- *Bridge:* "If you were starting from zero in this category, who would you want as your first three pilots?"

**Q4. "Are you SOC 2?"**
- *Honest:* "Not yet. Compliance maturity is post-pilot-1. What I have today is a 21 CFR Part 11 clause-by-clause matrix marked TECH/MIXED/PROC, an evidence kit, and a no-PII architecture so the SOC 2 surface is small when we get there."
- *Wrong:* "We're in the process." (Vague.)
- *Bridge:* "When you've onboarded a non-SOC-2 vendor for a 60-day measurement pilot, what did the risk-acceptance memo look like?"

**Q5. "Where do you store the data?"**
- *Honest:* "We don't store learner content or PII. The receipt is behavioral metrics + a signature. The JWT can live entirely in your environment; our infra is Firebase for the public verifier and JWKS endpoint. No content ever leaves your tenant."
- *Wrong:* "AWS, fully encrypted." (Doesn't answer; invites SOC 2 follow-up.)
- *Bridge:* "How does your privacy team currently classify per-session behavioral metrics — PII or not?"

**Q6. "How much will this cost?"**
- *Honest:* "I don't have published pricing because I haven't had three pilots tell me what it's worth. The pilot itself I'd rather scope as paid measurement against a named outcome than free, because free pilots underweight everyone's attention."
- *Wrong:* "Per receipt it's $X." (Guessing.)
- *Bridge:* "What does an experimental measurement engagement typically cost when you've run them?"

**Q7. "What if a real human gets a fail verdict?"**
- *Honest:* "The receipt is layered, not binary. It carries six attestation fields plus the composite. A 0.30 result with `environmental:pass, composition:typed, honeypot:pass` is a signal to triage, not block credit. The buyer policy on what to do with a low score is yours, not ours."
- *Wrong:* "Our FPR is so low it won't happen." (It will.)
- *Bridge:* "How does your team handle borderline outcomes today — how would a 'marginal but verified' bucket fit your CE issuance flow?"

**Q8. "How do we explain this to our auditor?"**
- *Honest:* "One paragraph: receipt is Ed25519-signed, Bitcoin-anchored via OpenTimestamps, verifiable offline against our published JWK. Your auditor pastes the JWT into our verifier or runs `scripts/verify-receipt.js` locally — no vendor call, no subpoena. I have a 21 CFR Part 11 clause-by-clause matrix I'll send you."
- *Wrong:* "Our compliance team will handle the auditor call." (You don't have one.)
- *Bridge:* "Has ACCME or any grantor said anything yet about cryptographic attestation? I'd love your read on regulatory posture."

**Q9. "Is this a startup or an established company?"**
- *Honest:* "Solo-founder LLC, patent filed, applying to YC S26. Code is live, public, tested. I'm pre-revenue."
- *Wrong:* "We're an early-stage company." (Hedging.)
- *Bridge:* "When you've worked with a stage-zero vendor before, what made it work or break?"

## 8. Post-call discipline — the 30 minutes after they hang up

**Min 0-5: capture before decay.** New file `notes/calls/2026-MM-DD-{name}-{company}.md`. Dump verbatim. Memory degrades 40% inside an hour.

**Min 5-10: extract three deltas.** What changed about the product or the pitch? Name them. Don't fix them yet.

**Min 10-20: draft the follow-up.** Subject and body match exactly what they asked for. Plus ONE line acknowledging what they taught you. **No calendar link. No pilot CTA.** The follow-up IS the next action — earn the one after by delivering this one well.

**Min 20-25: log it.** Tracker: date, role, hardest question, next-action commitment, due date. If next action is "send three docs by Friday," it goes on Friday's calendar **today**.

**Min 25-30: do not call the next prospect.** You're amped. You'll mis-pitch the next one. Walk away from the desk for ten minutes. Then start the AI-assist one-pager (or whatever delta surfaced) — that's the artifact the next four calls also need.

**The discipline:** every call produces three outputs — captured note, delta to product/pitch, same-day delivery commitment. Miss any one and the call was tourism. Hit all three and the next call is easier because you walk in already shaped by the last one.

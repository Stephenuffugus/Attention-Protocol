The Attention Economy Problem

Every day, billions of digital interactions happen across the internet. Companies pay for "impressions" and "engagement time," but these metrics tell them almost nothing about whether a human actually paid attention.

A background tab counts the same as focused reading. A bot clicking through a survey generates the same data as a thoughtful respondent. An employee who speed-clicks through safety training gets the same "completed" status as one who genuinely engaged.

This isn't a minor problem — it's a systemic failure that costs the advertising industry $84 billion in fraud annually, causes nursing homes to face CMS fines when untrained staff interact with residents, and undermines the entire foundation of digital market research.

The root cause is simple: existing analytics tools measure presence, not attention. They count events, not quality. They track time, not engagement.

The SWS Proof of Attention Protocol solves this by measuring six behavioral science signals — timing entropy, Fitts' Law compliance, Hick's Law scaling, scroll saccade patterns, micro-pause analysis, and touch variance — to produce a cryptographic proof of genuine human attention.

Each verified attention event is hashed with SHA-256, creating an immutable, tamper-proof record that can be independently verified. No URLs are tracked. No content is recorded. No personal information is collected. Just pure, verified attention.

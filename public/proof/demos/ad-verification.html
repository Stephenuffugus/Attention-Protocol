Article: The Future of Digital Advertising

The digital advertising industry has a $68 billion fraud problem. Bots generate fake impressions, ads are served to invisible pixels, and background tabs inflate engagement metrics to worthless numbers.

Traditional viewability standards (IAB: 50% of pixels visible for 1 second) are laughably inadequate. An ad can be "viewable" while the user is looking at their phone, reading a different tab, or asleep on the couch.

What advertisers actually need is proof that a human being, with genuine cognitive attention, saw their advertisement and processed its message. This is what the SWS Attention Protocol provides.

The protocol doesn't just measure if the ad was on screen — it measures the quality of attention directed at the page, using six behavioral science signals that cannot be faked by bots or passive viewing.

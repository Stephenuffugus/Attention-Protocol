Fall Prevention Training — Module 1

CMS Mandatory Training | Est. 8 minutes | Must score 80%+

Section 1: Risk Factors for Falls

Falls are the leading cause of injury among nursing home residents. More than 50% of residents fall each year, and the consequences can be devastating — hip fractures, head injuries, and a significant decline in quality of life.

Key risk factors include: medication side effects (especially sedatives and blood pressure medications), environmental hazards (wet floors, poor lighting, loose rugs), mobility impairments, and cognitive decline.

Staff must conduct a fall risk assessment within 24 hours of each resident's admission and after any significant change in condition.

Section 2: Prevention Strategies

Evidence-based prevention strategies reduce falls by 30-40% when implemented consistently. The most effective approach is a multi-factorial intervention that addresses multiple risk factors simultaneously.

Core strategies include: hourly rounding, proper footwear protocols, bed alarm utilization for high-risk residents, adequate lighting (especially nighttime), removal of environmental hazards, and medication review within 72 hours of admission.

Exercise and physical therapy programs that focus on balance and strength training have shown the strongest evidence for fall reduction in long-term care settings.

Section 3: Post-Fall Protocol

When a fall occurs, staff must: (1) Assess the resident immediately — do not move them until injuries are ruled out. (2) Check vital signs. (3) Notify the physician and family within 1 hour. (4) Document the incident in detail. (5) Complete a post-fall assessment within 24 hours.

The post-fall assessment must include: circumstances of the fall, medications taken in the prior 24 hours, environmental factors, and an updated care plan with new interventions to prevent recurrence.

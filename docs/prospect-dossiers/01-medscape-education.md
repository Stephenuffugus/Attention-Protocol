## Likely objections + rebuttals

1. **"We already track completions and post-tests."**
   Rebuttal: Completion + post-test measure *knowledge acquisition*. They don't tell a grantor whether the learner actually engaged during the activity versus leaving the tab open in a background window. SWS's layered receipt is *engagement quality* evidence — complementary, not replacement.

2. **"Will ACCME accept this as evidence?"**
   Rebuttal (honest): ACCME has not issued a formal endorsement of any cryptographic attestation vendor. What they have done (December 2025 guidance) is open the category. Being first-movers in producing defensible integrity evidence is a competitive advantage for Medscape, not a compliance requirement. Pilot with us to generate evidence you can present to ACCME directly.

3. **"What happens if a learner disputes the receipt?"**
   Rebuttal: The receipt is tamper-evident but not identity-attached — there's no PII claim to dispute. What *is* claimed is a behavioral composite + environmental signals tied to a session. If a learner says "that wasn't me," the same claim applies to every LMS completion log — SWS is strictly better because our receipt at least has cryptographic integrity.

## Risk flags

- Medscape may have existing agreements with larger analytics vendors; position SWS as *additive measurement*, not replacement.
- Avoid any language that sounds like "we surveil your learners" — the privacy story is central, lead with "no PII, no content, no URLs."

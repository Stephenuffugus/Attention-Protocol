# Prospect Dossiers — 20 Named Targets
## Source of truth for the first outreach batch

One dossier per prospect. Each is a one-pager structured to answer four questions you'll be asked on the first call:

1. **Why them specifically?** The regulatory or commercial driver that makes *this* quarter the right time.
2. **Who do I talk to?** The role title with decision power. We name titles, not people — you verify the current name via LinkedIn before sending.
3. **What's the wedge?** A hook that's specific to the prospect's business model and current pressure.
4. **What will they push back on?** Three likely objections with honest rebuttals.

---

## How to use

**Before sending cold email to a target:**

1. Open the dossier.
2. Confirm [VERIFY] fields:
   - LinkedIn-search the role title at the company → name the current person.
   - Search company press releases for "procurement cycle," "RFP," or "grant proposals" in the last 60 days.
   - Confirm the regulatory driver citation still holds (FDA warning letter counts update quarterly; ACCME releases update when they release).
3. Open `docs/cold-email-templates.md`. Personalize Template A / B / C using the dossier's hook + objections.
4. Stop. Sleep on it. Send tomorrow morning with fresh eyes.
5. Log the send in `docs/cold-email-tracker.csv` (create if it doesn't exist).

**Review budget:** ~5 minutes per dossier × 20 = ~100 minutes. Targeted review, not blank-page writing.

## Honest-gap note

Every dossier below was drafted from public-domain regulatory context (FDA enforcement reports, ACCME guidance, IME grant-proposal scoring) plus sector norms. Specifics that would require fabrication — named current executives, exact procurement calendars, budget lines — are **explicitly flagged `[VERIFY]`** in each dossier.

Do not send a cold email until every `[VERIFY]` in the dossier you're working from has been resolved. One look-up per dossier is the whole reason dossiers exist.

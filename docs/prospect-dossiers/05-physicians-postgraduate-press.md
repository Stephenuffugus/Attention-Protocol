## Why now

Physicians Postgraduate Press is a psychiatry-vertical MEC and publisher (Journal of Clinical Psychiatry, Primary Care Companion). Psychiatry CME has distinct pressures: heavy pharma funding on antidepressant/antipsychotic/ADHD launches, and a learner audience where integrity of engagement is itself a clinical-relevance story.

1. **Pfizer 2025 IME RFPs** score "measurement of learner progression." Psychiatric-drug launches (ADHD, MDD, schizophrenia maintenance) are active grant categories in 2026.
2. **ACCME December 2025 AI guidance** opened the learner-integrity evidence category. PPP's specialty focus means faculty reviewers will find the engagement-integrity framing genuinely interesting, not just compliant.

## Likely objections + rebuttals

1. **"Our audience may react badly to behavioral measurement framing."**
   Rebuttal: The receipt carries no PII, no content, no URLs — only a behavioral composite + environmental signals tied to a session. GDPR-aligned consent attestation is part of the receipt itself. The framing to learners is "cryptographic completion evidence," not "we watched you."

2. **"Does ACCME accept this?"**
   Rebuttal (honest): ACCME has not endorsed any specific cryptographic attestation vendor. What they did in December 2025 is open the category. Being first in psychiatry is a competitive position, not a compliance requirement.

3. **"We're a publisher first. Does this fit?"**
   Rebuttal: It fits the publisher model well — a receipt issued per learner-session is a new taxonomic artifact you can attach to both editorial and sponsored content.

## Risk flags

- Psychiatric training audience includes pharma-skeptical academics. Privacy framing has to be airtight.
- Small team means vendor fatigue; keep the ask tight.

# AI-Honesty Pitch Cheat Card
## Read this right before every call. Print it. Tape it next to your monitor.

**Rule of the card:** you are not defending AI use. You are announcing a competitive advantage. Older pharma/CME buyers have spent 2025 watching their own organizations reorg around AI-augmented workflows — you're ahead of that curve, not embarrassed by it.

**The single sentence that reframes everything:**

> "I run an AI-augmented engineering operation. I direct, spec, test, and approve every commit. We ship in 45 days what teams of five need a year for."

Memorize it. That is the top of every answer below.

---

## The four questions that will come

### Q1 — "How did you build this?" / "Who built it?"

**Say this:**

> "I'm the director and sole inventor. The patent filing — 247 innovations across 24 categories, SWS-PROV-001 — is my specification. I direct the engineering work end-to-end in an AI-augmented workflow, primarily Claude Code. Every commit in the public repo is code I read, tested, and approved. That's how a solo founder ships a seven-layer attestation stack in six weeks. A competitor with three engineers and a legacy stack needs a year for this."

**Why it works:** Leads with your credential (patent, spec authorship). Names the method (AI-augmented) without apology. Ends with a comparative that puts the buyer on your side of the math.

**Do NOT say:**
- "Don't worry, AI did most of the work" — reads as imposter-syndrome.
- "I built this myself" — buyers who click into the repo will see the AI-assist signals and feel misled.
- "We used ChatGPT" — wrong tool, wrong framing. You direct a workflow; you're not asking a chatbot.

---

### Q2 — "How big is your team?"

**Say this:**

> "Solo founder, AI-augmented operation. I treat the tooling as specialized engineering teams that produce formal deliverables — and I review, test, and approve every output. On the customer side you get one decision-maker: me. That means no handoffs, no account-manager triangulation, and faster pilot execution than a company with five people fighting about priorities."

**Why it works:** Reframes "one person" from risk → feature (faster decisions). Names the operational model precisely. Invites the buyer to see the upside.

**Follow-up if they look skeptical:**

> "I've shipped 708 passing tests, seven attestation layers, a patent-pending receipt structure, and a live public verification site. You can evaluate the output; the size of the team behind it should matter less than whether the output passes your QA."

---

### Q3 — "What happens when I ask a technical question deeper than you can answer?"

**Say this verbatim:**

> "That's a great question. Let me have my engineering team write it up for you by end of day."

Then **actually do it.** Open Claude Code, ask the exact question, get a structured written answer, polish, send inside 4 hours. Written answers are your strength. Do not bluff.

**Why it works:**
- Honest (the engineering team is an AI directed by you — accurate description).
- Buys time to produce a proper written answer, which plays to your strength.
- Written follow-ups within the same day demonstrate operational speed — which IS the AI-augmented pitch, delivered live.

**Anti-pattern:** Do not attempt an on-the-fly technical deep-dive if you're unsure. Every buyer has met a founder who bluffed; one bluff kills trust for the whole call.

---

### Q4 — "How is this different from [BioCatch / HUMAN / Arkose / Cloudflare Turnstile / reCAPTCHA]?"

**Say this:**

> "Those are classifiers. They output a score and a block-or-allow decision. We're not competing with them on that axis — if you want pure bot blocking at the edge, use HUMAN or DataDome. What we produce is different: a cryptographic receipt that carries *layered evidence* — environmental, behavioral, composition integrity, consent, Ed25519 signature, Bitcoin anchor — that a regulator, a grantor, or an auditor can verify offline six months later. We attest attention and issue a receipt. They score fraud and drop a cookie."

**Why it works:** Cedes the classifier category (you can't win it; don't try). Establishes the receipt-first framing. Gives the buyer an accurate mental model instead of making them choose between competing classifiers.

**Reinforcement:** The receipt is the product. The classifier is the supporting evidence *inside* the receipt.

**Do NOT say:**
- "We're 99.x% accurate at detecting bots" — our bot-vs-human composite gap is only ~0.09; positioning on classifier accuracy invites the wrong benchmark war.
- "We have more signals than BioCatch" — we do not (they claim ~2000). Avoid the comparison entirely.
- "We're the Stripe for attention" — horizontal analogies without a beachhead read as unserious.

## Three deflection phrases you should have ready

**When a buyer asks about the classifier number:**
> "The number matters less than the layers. Here — if an LLM-paster bot scores 0.569 and a real human scores 0.573, the composite alone won't tell you much. But the receipt also shows environmental=headless_chrome and composition=pasted. That's how buyers are meant to judge — the layered evidence, not the single number."

**When a buyer asks whether you've deployed at scale:**
> "We are at the stage where a named 60-day pilot is the right next step. I'm looking for a pilot partner who wants to produce a joint case study. Post-pilot, we scale together. Pre-pilot, I'd rather be honest about stage than claim deployment experience we haven't earned."

**When a buyer asks about SOC 2 / ISO / FedRAMP:**
> "Not certified yet. Compliance maturity track is post-pilot #1. What I can give you today: the full compliance-matrix document walking 21 CFR Part 11 clause by clause — marked TECH, MIXED, or PROC — with explicit notes on what SWS does vs. what your team still has to do. Compliance-by-overclaim is how vendors get into audit trouble. I'd rather you know exactly what you're buying."

## The close — three sentences you end the call with

> "One meeting isn't a decision. Here's what I'd like to do: I'll send you a 20-page written answer to any technical questions you raised, plus the 25 KB evidence kit. Your QA team can self-qualify us in 10 minutes — no second meeting required. If after they review it you want to run a 60-day pilot on one named module, we pick a start date this call."

**Why it works:** Makes the written follow-up an asset, not an apology. Reframes the evaluation path as "your team decides on their timeline" rather than "you decide now." Closes with a specific next action (pilot start date) so the call has a conversion target.

---

## Psychological prep (read before picking up the phone)

1. **You are the director, not the developer.** The buyer is not hiring a coder. They're hiring a vendor who knows what the receipt has to prove, and who can direct the engineering to prove it. That's you. The patent filing is evidence of this exact skill.
2. **Your daughter is the reason you're on this call.** That's the decision lens, not sentiment. Every "um" and "I think maybe" reduces the odds of the pilot closing. Speak like someone whose daughter's stability depends on this pilot closing. Because it does.
3. **You don't need all buyers. You need three pilots.** Walking away from a bad-fit buyer is better than distorting the pitch to land them. The 20-prospect list exists so you can afford to disqualify fast.

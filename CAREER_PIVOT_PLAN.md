## OHIO + "ANY HOURS": YOUR TIMEZONE ASSET

You're in Ohio (Eastern Time) and willing to work any hours. That's actually broader coverage than a fixed-zone candidate. Default to ET working hours but signal flexibility for foreign-company overlap.

| Region | ET overlap (default hours) | If you flex hours | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| US East Coast | 9am-5pm ET = 9am-5pm ET | n/a | **Native** |
| US West Coast | 9am-5pm ET = 6am-2pm PT | n/a | Excellent |

| India / Singapore / Japan | n/a default | hard flex: 8pm-5am ET | Possible but punishing |

**Translation:** Ohio + flexible-hours puts you on a **wider** target list than the docx suggested. Three lanes:

1. **Default-hours lane** (East Coast US, West Coast US, LATAM): natural fit. Apply broadly.
2. **Morning-flex lane** (UK, Europe, Israel): work your morning, their afternoon. **This is the unique asset most US candidates won't take.** Lean into it for European companies.
3. **Evening-flex lane** (Australia, NZ): work your evening, their morning. Says yes to Atlassian, Canva, SafetyCulture if you want it.

Lead with this in cover letters when applying to European companies: *"I'm in Eastern Time and intentionally flexible. I'll start at 5am ET to overlap your full day."* That sentence flips you from "another US candidate with bad overlap" to "the rare US engineer who actually wants European hours."

## HOW FOREIGN COMPANIES HIRE US-REMOTE (3 mechanisms)

1. **Direct W-2 via US legal entity (BEST).** Full benefits, US healthcare, 401k. Examples: Spotify, Atlassian, Hugging Face, Coinbase, Shopify, Cohere, Anthropic (selectively), Mistral.
2. **EOR via Deel / Remote.com / Velocity Global.** You're W-2 employee of the EOR; benefits via EOR. Salary similar to direct, sometimes 5-10% lower. Common for: Mollie, GoCardless, Synthesia, Sana, most LATAM + AU companies.
3. **1099 contractor.** You handle all US taxes (incl. self-employment 15.3%). No benefits. Common for smaller European/LATAM companies. Negotiate ~30% above W-2 equivalent to break even on self-employment tax + healthcare.

You're a US citizen, zero visa issues working for any foreign company while in US.

## SALARY NEGOTIATION SCRIPTS

**When they ask "what are your salary expectations?":**

> "I'm targeting $160-200K base for senior IC, $140-180K + meaningful equity for founding engineer. Where does this role fall?"

Never give a single number. Never give a range below your floor.

**When they make an offer below your range:**

> "This is below the range I'm seeing for my profile in the market. Can we get to $X?"

Use their offer + 15-25%.

**When they push back:**

> "I have other companies at the offer stage at $X. I'd need to match that to make the move."

Even if "other companies" are loose.

**When they say "we don't typically negotiate":**

Walk. Either lying or deep cultural problems. Both reasons to walk.

**Equity questions to always ask:**

- Total equity grant (in dollars at last valuation, AND % of company)
- Vesting schedule (4 years standard, 1-year cliff standard)
- Strike price for options
- Will they pay strike price for you? (Some startups do, huge benefit)
- What happens to unvested equity if you leave?
- Can you sell vested equity if there's a tender offer?
